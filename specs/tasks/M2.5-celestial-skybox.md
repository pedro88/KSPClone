# M2.5 — Celestial skybox — code-ready tasks

> Slice added after M2 was merged: a procedural skybox that renders the three
> *celestial bodies* (Sun / Earth / Moon) as they appear from the controlled
> vessel. Companion to M2-T12 (which only added the data; this is the visual
> counterpart). Each ticket cites the spec requirement(s) it satisfies. Terms
> in *italics* are in [CONTEXT.md](../../CONTEXT.md).

> **Constitutional anchors.** Art. 2 — render is a separate concern, decoupled
> from the sim cadence. Art. 4 — the *living universe* is the universe, not the
> what-you-see window: the skybox is a presentation layer on top of authoritative
> state, never a source of truth.

## Slice 2.5.1 — Procedural skybox shell

### M2.5-T01 — Procedural skybox shell with body billboards
- **Satisfies:** ORBIT-1 (visualisation support), NET-4 (rendering layer)
- **Depends on:** M2-T12 (Sun added to `BodyRegistry`)
- **Intent:** Give the client a presentation layer that draws the three *celestial bodies* as seen from the *controlled vessel*, without touching the sim core or any server code. No textures — shader procedural only.
- **Touches:** new `Assets/Scripts/Client/CelestialSkyboxRenderer.cs`; new `Assets/Shaders/CelestialBody.shader` (procedural unlit/colour).
- **Steps:**
  1. Create `CelestialSkyboxRenderer` as a separate object from `ClientWorldRenderer`. It owns: (a) an inverted-sphere "skybox shell" parented to the controlled vessel, (b) one inner mesh per body (Sun/Earth/Moon).
  2. Each body mesh sits at `(BodyRegistry.WorldPositionOf(body, gameTime) − cameraWorldPos)`, normalised to a unit direction and a chosen radius on the inner shell (e.g. 100 m). Apparent angular size: real physical angle (2·atan(R / D)), floored to a configurable minimum (default ~2°). Sun glow radius follows an outer shell at the same direction with a larger radius.
  3. Procedural shader (no textures):
     - Sun: emissive radial gradient yellow→white→orange-red, animated FBM noise for surface granulation, billboard glow halo on outer shell.
     - Earth: gradient blue→white procedural (simple latitude bands + slow FBM), no continents in M2.5 (lands later).
     - Moon: grey procedural with low-frequency noise, slightly darker on the "night" half via simple Lambertian dot-product with sun direction.
  4. Wire up in `ClientBootstrap.LateUpdate` after `_renderer.Render(...)` so the skybox rides the floating origin of the vessel.
  5. Sun direction (used by the Moon shading) is `BodyRegistry.WorldPositionOf(Sun, t) − cameraWorldPos`, normalised.
- **Acceptance (manual, in-editor):**
  - Launch scene, hold altitude on the pad → Sun disk visible in some direction (yellow-orange), with a soft glow.
  - Spin the orbit camera 360° → Earth and Moon appear at their expected relative directions (Sun offset from Earth by ~1 AU along its orbital position; Moon offset from Earth by ~MoonOrbitRadius).
  - Walking around with `Time.timeScale = 1000` → bodies visibly drift (Earth orbits Sun once per ~3.16e7 s, scaled by warp).
  - At default warp 1×, no visual jitter, no float32 glitches at the skybox shell radius.
- **Est:** M

## Slice 2.5.2 — Apparent-size policy (design decision carried forward)

The "hybrid" apparent-size policy (real angle with a minimum) is captured here
so future contributors know where to look:

> Apparent angular radius = max(atan(bodyRadius / distance), minAngularRadius)
> where minAngularRadius defaults to ~2° (≈35 mrad).

If you want a different policy later (KSP-style fixed ~3°), the only place to
touch is the constant in `CelestialSkyboxRenderer`. The sim core never reads
visual apparent size.

## Slice 2.5.3 — Surface launch + ground contact

> Added after the skybox landed: the demo craft starts *on Earth's surface* and
> rests there under real weight instead of floating ~50 Earth radii out. First
> collision in the project. Scoped by [ADR-0018](../../docs/adr/0018-surface-as-local-tangent-plane-radial-up.md):
> flat tangent-plane surface, landed craft is active-physics, launch site at the
> body's +Y pole so radial-up = world +Y (no client surface-frame work).

### M2.5-T02 — Ground contact for a surface-launched vessel
- **Satisfies:** PHYS-7 (surface ground contact + weight), PHYS-1/2/4 (active-physics bubble, rigid vessel)
- **Depends on:** M1 bubble/physics stack (`BubbleIntegrator`, `ServerVesselBodies`, `UnityBubbleHost`), M2-T12 (Earth orbits Sun)
- **Intent:** The seed craft spawns resting on Earth's surface at the +Y pole, held up by a server-side static ground collider, and can throttle up off the pad. No terrain, no landed-on-rails.
- **Touches:**
  - `Assets/Scripts/SimCore/WorldSeed.cs` — surface spawn elements (circular, `e=0`, `i=0`, `Ω=0`, `argp=π/2`, `M0=0`, `r=EarthRadius+halfHeight`) so the craft sits at `EarthCentre + (0, r, 0)`.
  - `Assets/Scripts/Server/Bubble/RigidVesselBody.cs` — add a `CapsuleCollider` sized to the seed craft so it can generate contact.
  - new `Assets/Scripts/Server/Bubble/SurfaceGroundBody.cs` (+ host wiring) — a static ground-plane collider placed in the landed craft's bubble scene at the pad, normal +Y.
  - `Assets/Scripts/Server/ServerBootstrap.cs` — `_demoStartOnSurface` mode: seed surface elements, spawn the ground plane on promotion, zero velocity (reuse `OnDemoPromoted`).
- **Steps:**
  1. `WorldSeed.CreateSurfaceVessel()` (or params) → circular element at the +Y pole; `WorldSeed.SurfaceSpawnRadius`, pad half-height constants.
  2. Give `RigidVesselBody` a capsule collider (radius ~1 m, height ~4 m) on `Awake`; keep the explicit inertia tensor in `ServerVesselBodies` (collider must not silently reset it).
  3. `SurfaceGroundBody`: a static `BoxCollider` (wide + thin, top face at the pad) created in the bubble's `PhysicsScene`, positioned in bubble-local coords at spawn. No `Rigidbody`. Not rebased (ADR-0018 §4).
  4. `ServerBootstrap`: when `_demoStartOnSurface`, seed the surface elements; on `VesselPromoted`, create the ground plane under the craft and zero its velocity.
  5. Gravity is the existing point-mass `ApplyGravity` — at `r ≈ EarthRadius` it is ~9.82 m/s², so the craft has real weight with no new gravity code.
- **Acceptance:**
  - **EditMode (SimCore, no Unity):** the surface spawn elements evaluate to Earth-frame `(0, EarthRadius+halfHeight, 0)` at epoch; radial-up (world pos − Earth world pos, normalised) = `(0, 1, 0)` within tol; distance from Earth centre = `EarthRadius + halfHeight`.
  - **PlayMode (in-editor, pending run):** craft spawns on the pad, rests without sinking or drifting; with throttle it lifts off; released it settles back. No jitter at rest.
- **Est:** M
- **Note:** PlayMode/PhysX contact can only be validated in the editor (same caveat carried by M1/M2). SimCore-testable parts are covered in EditMode; contact behaviour is validated on the next in-editor run.
